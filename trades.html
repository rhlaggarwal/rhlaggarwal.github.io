Why are number of trades so high?

The idea behind screening for trades through code is to breeze through chart patterns and get down to fewer names. Our aim is to cater to trend and momentum traders who are looking for breakout stocks. We can tighten our levers but that would mean no action for longer intervals. We believe current signals are optimized to give enough options to users.

Is this a strategy?

No - we would rather call it a framework. We believe if you can filter down through automated rules and chose trades out of this set, your probability of finding winning trades go higher. Some traders like to run fewer positions with bigger sizes. Some prefer to hold for longer. The framework increases your chances to find stocks in trend quickly and accurately. It will also help to stay on top of all stocks at one single place.

How about rules behind the framework of longs-shorts?

We consider the following metrics to screen stocks

Technical parameters:

  1. Past return performance
  2. Exponential moving averages
  3. Momentum
  4. ATR (average true range)
  5. Proximity to 52-week highs for longs and 52-week lows for shorts

Fundamental filters:

  1. Debt-equity ratios
  2. Return on Equity

Why do we give user choices?
