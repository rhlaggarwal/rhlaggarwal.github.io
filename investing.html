Below chart compares performance of SIPs in 1)Fixed deposit 2)Nifty ETFs and 3) Price to Earning(PE) based asset allocation strategy. The strategy decides whether to invest in Nifty ETFs or Fixed deposits based on how expensive Nifty is. The decision is taken on a monthly basis. You either stay in fixed deposit or equity at a particular time.

The numbers in the graph is calculated using starting monthly savings of Rs 10,000 from 1999, adjusted for inflation(6%) since then. The strategy exited equities whenever price to earnings level was very high end compared to historical averages.

Nifty trades at a PE level of 25 as of Oct 2018. Strategy advises to stay in fixed deposits but triggers are close to re-enter equities. People may use dips to buy. However strictly going by strategy one should wait. We will update signals next by Nov end.



Quantify India in its effort to mathematically explain concepts has something on gold investing too. Below graph assumes a monthly SIP of Rs 1000 from 1980 (increased by 6% annually). The current SIP amount in 2018 would be Rs 9700 and total investment outlay Rs 17.6Lakhs. The current market value of total gold holdings would be whopping Rs 84L. That’s an annual return of 9.38%.

Key points to note

  • The table above displays the annual return details of Gold and Nifty
  • For example - if you invest in Nifty the range of returns you can earn in 10years are from 6% to 20% with an average of 13% and a standard deviation of 2% (quite low)
  • Notice how standard deviations reduce with longer term of investments
  • Also since the range in even 10 years is so varied - its always advisable to time diversify your investments using SIP
  • Lump sum investments at high valuations should be strictly avoided
  • Equities give better returns than gold over longer run but with higher volatility
