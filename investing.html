What does the strategy do?

The strategy decides whether to invest in Nifty ETFs or fixed deposits based on how expensive Nifty is. The decision is taken on a monthly basis. You invest in fixed deposit or equity at the end of the month. This strategy falls under Dynamic Asset Allocation in investing parlance.

The numbers in the chart are calculated using monthly savings of Rs 10,000 starting from 1999. The savings are increased at 6% inflation every year. Latest SIP amount is Rs 35,852.

The strategy exits equities whenever Price to Earning level is very high compared to historical averages.

Current scenario (May 2020)

Nifty currently trades at a PE level of 22+ and strategy advises to stay 100% in equities. The strategy shifted back into equities from Fixed deposits in end of Mar’2020 after a sharp market fall. Key points to note

  • The table above displays the annual return details of Gold and Nifty
  • For example - if you invest in Nifty the range of returns you can earn in 10years are from 6% to 20% with an average of 13% and a standard deviation of 2% (quite low)
  • Notice how standard deviations reduce with longer term of investments
  • Also since the range in even 10 years is so varied - its always advisable to time diversify your investments using SIP
  • Lumpsum investments at high valuations should be avoided strictly
  • Equities give better returns than gold over longer run but with higher volatility
